📖 The Q1 Story

Cushie did $1.04M USD equivalent in Q1 across 4 countries and 2 products:

• 🇺🇸 US (cushiehome.com): $586K USD — beat forecast by $10K overall, Cushie V2 missed target (-12%), Snuggle smashed it (+124%)
• 🇦🇺 AU (lifely.com.au): $445K AUD — Cushie V2 MISSED target by -24%, Snuggle a bright spot (not in Q1 target)
• 🇨🇦 CA (lifely.com.au): $28K AUD — Cushie barely started, Snuggle breaking out (2→5→18 orders)
• 🇳🇿 NZ (lifely.com.au): $8K AUD — declining, not a priority

The model target for Q1 Cushie V2 AU alone was $430K AUD. We did $282K. That's a -34% miss. Meanwhile Snuggle, which wasn't even in the original AU model, did $163K AUD unplanned.

🔑 Cross-Country Product Insights

🛋️ Cushie MER is consistent at ~6.5x in US and AU — product-market fit travels. CA/NZ too small to judge.
🛏️ Snuggle MER is consistent at ~3.7x in US and AU — same efficiency problem exists in both markets. Not a market issue, a product/creative issue.
🇨🇦 CA Snuggle is the outlier — 6x MER on tiny spend. Either early promise or statistical noise. Worth a real budget test.
💰 Cushie generates 73% of total gross revenue ($925K of $1.28M AUD) with only 60% of the Meta spend. It's the efficient scale lever.
⚠️ Snuggle is 30% of revenue but 40% of spend — the inverse. Every dollar we move from Snuggle to Cushie improves blended MER.
