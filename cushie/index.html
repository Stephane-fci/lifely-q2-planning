Cushie — Q2 Planning

Two stores. Two products. Four regions. Here's the picture.

📖 The Story

Cushie is two products: Cushie Modular (the original sleeper sofa) and Snuggle (the newer sofa bed). They sell through two stores: cushiehome.com (US, USD) and lifely.com.au (AU/CA/NZ, AUD).

Q1 reality: Cushie Modular is the cash machine — 819 orders, 6.5x MER in both markets. Snuggle is growing fast but burning efficiency — 258 orders, only 3.8x MER. Same brand, very different unit economics.

The pattern is consistent across markets: Cushie Modular outperforms Snuggle on MER by ~70% in both US and AU. But Snuggle has higher AOV ($1,511 vs $1,086) and is the only product breaking through in Canada.

❓ The Q2 Question

Cushie Modular is the proven engine. The question is: how much can we scale spend before efficiency drops? 6.5x MER gives a lot of headroom.

Snuggle is the growth bet. The question is: can we fix the post-click experience and creative fatigue before the math stops working? March US Snuggle MER of 2.48x is below most reasonable margin profiles.

Today: Where do we put the next $50K — into proven Cushie scale, or into fixing Snuggle so it can scale?

🌟 The bright spot: Canada — Mar saw 11 Snuggle CA orders, 5.3x MER. Fastest-growing region for any product.
🎯 What would help: Fix US post-click experience, find the one creative that breaks the pattern, or pull spend back to where MER works (~$10K/mo)
